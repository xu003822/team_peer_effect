Instructions

In this study, you will be in a randomly formed group of {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Each participant in the group is given {{ Constants.endowment }}. The group has the opportunity to undertake a joint project. The game has two rounds. In the first round, each participant in the group decides how much she or he is going to contribute to the project. Contribution could be any integer from 0 to {{ Constants.endowment }}.

In the second round, each participant in the group will be asked to indicate their contribution levels for each of the 21 possible average contribution levels of their groupmates.

The experimenter will randomly select three participants in a group and those players' payoff depends on the decisions they made in the first round. The fourth player's payoff will be determined by his second round decision when the average contribution levels of the other group members equals that of the first round. The earnings from the project are calculated as follows: The contributions of all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants are added up, the total contribution is multiplied by a factor of {{ Constants.multiplier }}, and the resulting amount is the total earnings from the project, which is evenly split among all {{ Constants.players_per_group }} participants. Your payoff in each round equals your earnings from the project, plus the amount you did not contribute.
