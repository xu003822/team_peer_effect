Welcome. This exercise simulates a situation where a group of individuals must make decisions on how to use a shared resource --- e.g., a forest, a water source, or a fishery. You have been selected to participate in a group of five volunteers among those signed up for playing. At the end of the game, you will be able to earn cash. Game Description:

This activity has 18 rounds. At the beginning of each round, your group shares a fishery of 200 fish. In each round, each group member must decide how many fish between 0 and 40 (inclusive) to catch. This means you can catch 0, or 1, or 2, .., or 40 fish. These decisions will be made at the same time and no communication is allowed. At the end of the round, the number of fish doubles, up to the lake's capacity of 200 fish, and those fish are divided equally among the group members. For each fish caught you will receive 10 points

Payment rule:

Final payment will depend on the quantity of points that you accumulate over the rounds.

1 dollar is worth 40 points

Example:
Imagine that in a given round you decide to catch 10 fish and other group members catch a total of 40 fish. This results in 150 fish left in the lake. At the end of the round, the amount of fish doubles, up to the lakeâ€™s capacity of 200 fish. The 200 fish will be evenly distributed to the group members. Thus each group member will get 40 more fish at the end of the round. Therefore, your total amount of fish in this round would be 10 + 40 = 50.
