Welcome. This exercise attempts to create a situation where a group of individuals must make decisions on how to use a shared resource --- e.g., a forest, a water source, or a fishery. You have been selected to participate in a group of five people among those signed up for playing. At the end of the game, you will be able to earn cash. The cash prizes will depend on the quantity of points that you accumulate over the rounds.

Game Description:

This activity is divided into 18 rounds. At the beginning of each round, your group consists of five people share a fishery of 200 fish. In each round, each group member must decide how many fish between 0 and 40 to catch. This means you can catch 0, or 1, or 2, .., or 40 fish. These decisions will be made at the same time and no communication is allowed. At the end of the round, the number of fish doubles, up to the lake's capacity of 200 fish and is divided equally among the group members. For each fish caught you will receive 10 points

Payment rule:

1 dollar is worth 40 points

Example:
Imagine you decide to catch 10 fish in a round, and that total catch of other group members are 40 fish. This results in 150 fish left in the ocean. At the end of the round, the amount of fish doubles, up to the lakeâ€™s capacity of 200 fish. So there are 200 fish at the end of the round. The 200 fish will be evenly distributed to the group members, Thus, each group member will get 40 more fish at the end of the round. Therefore, your total amount of fish in this round would be 10 + 40 = 50.
